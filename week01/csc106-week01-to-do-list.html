Welcome to CSC 106! This week you will build the foundation for everything that follows in this course. You will read about Linux hardware concepts and where system data is stored, then put that knowledge to work by installing Ubuntu Server 24.04 LTS on a virtual machine on your own computer. The VM you create this week is not a one-time exercise — you will use it through Week 8.

Before you can install Linux, you need a hypervisor: VirtualBox if you are on Windows, or UTM if you are on a Mac with Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3/M4). The task list below is ordered to walk you through setup in a logical sequence. Complete the readings and the Cisco lab before installing your VM so you arrive at the installation with the relevant concepts fresh in mind.

The reading load is heavier this week than most — plan for 7–8 hours on the two assigned chapters alone. The hands-on installation and lab work add another 1–2 hours, and the discussion rounds out the week at approximately 9 hours total.

Complete all 15 tasks below in the order listed. Tasks with a point value are graded assignments. Point values are noted next to the task.

Linux Essentials Ch. 13 Lab — 15 pts Ubuntu Server Install — 25 pts RSI Discussion 1 — 15 pts Week Total — 55 pts
  1. Read the Week 01 Course Overview

    Open the Week 01 Course Overview page in Canvas. This page introduces the week's topics, learning objectives, and how everything connects to the course as a whole. Read it before doing anything else.

  2. Watch the Course Orientation and apt Demo Video

    "Welcome to CSC 106 and Package Management with apt" — a combined course orientation and live demo of apt commands. Watch this first to understand the structure of the course and what you will be doing with the package manager after your VM is installed. Approximately 12–15 minutes.

  3. Register for Cisco Networking Academy

    Go to the Cisco Networking Academy registration page and create your account. See the NetAcad Registration page in Canvas for step-by-step instructions, including important guidance on which email address to use.

  4. Enroll in the Required Course: Linux Essentials

    Use the invite link on the NetAcad Registration page to enroll in CSC106 Spring 2026 – Linux Essentials. This is your required course textbook and lab environment for CSC 106. The course opens March 23, 2026.

  5. Link Your NetAcad Account to NDG Online

    Inside the Linux Essentials course, open the Before You Get Started activity and follow the prompts to link your NDG account. You will need to create an NDG account if you do not already have one, then verify your email address. You must complete this step before you can access the Cisco labs.

  6. Read Linux Essentials Chapter 12: Understanding Computer Hardware

    Complete the full chapter reading in NetAcad. This chapter covers the hardware concepts that inform the decisions you will make when configuring your VM — CPU type, RAM, storage, and why architecture (x86_64 vs. ARM64) matters when choosing an ISO. Plan for approximately 3.5–4 hours.

  7. Read Linux Essentials Chapter 13: Where Data is Stored

    Complete the full chapter reading in NetAcad. Chapter 13 covers /proc, /var, /etc, package query commands, and process information — exactly the territory you will explore in the Cisco lab and on your own VM. Plan for approximately 3.5–4 hours.

  8. Complete the Linux Essentials Chapter 13 Lab 15 pts

    Launch the Chapter 13 lab in the Cisco VM environment (via NDG Online). You will explore /proc, use ps to view running processes, and practice package query commands including dpkg -l and dpkg -L. Submit your lab completion in Canvas.

  9. Watch the Hypervisor Setup Video for Your Platform

    Watch the video that matches your computer. Follow along as you watch — this is a walkthrough you do alongside the video.

    Windows "Download and Install VirtualBox on Windows" — covers downloading VirtualBox, running the installer, and verifying that virtualization (VT-x/AMD-V) is enabled in your BIOS.
    Mac (M-series) "Download and Install UTM on M-series Mac" — covers downloading UTM and completing initial setup on Apple Silicon hardware.
  10. Watch the Ubuntu Server Installation Video for Your Platform

    Watch the video that matches your hypervisor before you begin the installation. It narrates all key decision points: partitioning, user setup, enabling OpenSSH, and network configuration.

    Windows "Download and Install Ubuntu Server 24.04 on VirtualBox" — uses the standard x86_64 (amd64) ISO.
    Mac (M-series) "Download and Install Ubuntu Server 24.04 – ARM on UTM" — uses the ARM64 ISO. The two ISOs are not interchangeable.
  11. Install Your Hypervisor and Create a Virtual Machine

    Install VirtualBox (Windows) or UTM (Mac) on your computer, then create a new VM configured for a 64-bit Linux guest. Allocate at least 2 CPU cores, 2 GB RAM, and 20 GB of virtual disk space. If you run into trouble with VirtualBox and virtualization, the setup video covers the common BIOS fix. Post in the course Q&A forum early rather than waiting.

  12. Download and Install Ubuntu Server 24.04 LTS on Your VM

    Download the correct ISO for your platform (amd64 for VirtualBox/Windows; arm64 for UTM/Mac). Walk through the Ubuntu Server installer, making deliberate choices at each step: hostname, timezone, partitioning, initial user account, and enabling OpenSSH. There is no graphical desktop — a text-only login is normal and expected.

    Keep this VM. You will use it through Week 8. After installation, confirm it is working by running uname -a and checking network connectivity.

  13. Submit the Ubuntu Server Install Assignment 25 pts

    In Canvas, submit screenshots of your key installation steps (partitioning screen, user creation, and first login at the command line) plus a short write-up that explains the choices you made and why. Include terminal output showing uname -a and apt update.

  14. Post Your RSI Discussion 1 Original Post 15 pts Due Thursday

    In Canvas, respond to the Discussion 1 prompt: "Choosing and Installing Linux." Your original post must be at least 150 words and include at least one specific detail from your own installation experience. Post by Thursday of this week.

  15. Reply to at Least Two Classmates in Discussion 1 Due Sunday

    Return to Discussion 1 and post two substantive replies to classmates before Sunday. Each reply should add a new idea, ask a follow-up question, or offer a constructive alternative — not just "I agree." These replies are part of your Discussion 1 grade.
