Cisco Networking Academy Registration Guide

Setting up your NetAcad and NDG accounts for the course

CSC 106 uses the Cisco Networking Academy (NetAcad) platform as its primary textbook and lab environment. NetAcad provides structured, chapter-based reading, interactive exercises, and hands-on lab environments — all at no cost to you as a student at Mendocino College, which is a Cisco Academy member institution.

You will use NetAcad every week for readings and Cisco-hosted lab work. Setting up your account now, before the readings begin, ensures you will not lose time when lab assignments open.

Required

Linux Essentials

Your primary textbook for CSC 106. Covers 8 chapters across the semester. Chapters 12 and 13 are assigned in Week 1.

Optional

Linux 1 (LPIC-1)

Supplemental course aligned with the LPIC-1 certification. Used for extra credit reading and activities in several weeks.

Optional

Linux 2 (LPIC-1)

Advanced supplemental course. Used for extra credit activities later in the semester (Weeks 3, 6, 7, and 8).

NDG Online is the lab hosting platform linked to your NetAcad account. When you access Cisco labs, they run inside the NDG environment. You will need to link these two accounts — the steps below walk you through the process.

If you already have a Cisco Networking Academy account, you can skip to Step 2 and use your existing account to enroll in the course.

Important — use a personal email address, not your college email. Cisco keeps all of your course completions and certification records tied to the email address you register with. Once you graduate, your college email account is deactivated and you will lose access to your NetAcad history. Use a personal email address (Gmail, iCloud, etc.) that you expect to have long-term.
  1. 1
    Go to the Cisco Networking Academy website

    Open your browser and navigate to netacad.com .

  2. 2
    Click the "Login" button

    The Login button is in the upper-right corner of the page.

  3. 3
    Choose "Sign Up" to create a new account

    Below the login form, look for the link that reads "Don't have an account? Sign Up" and click it. If you already have an account, enter your email and password to log in instead.

  4. 4
    Fill in your registration information

    Enter your name, your personal email address (see the warning above), and create a password. Complete any verification steps Cisco requires (such as email confirmation) before proceeding.

  5. 5
    Confirm you can log in

    Log in to your new account and confirm you can access your NetAcad dashboard before moving on to Step 2.

    Tip: Save your NetAcad login credentials somewhere safe. You will use this account every week of the course and in future Cisco courses.

All students must enroll in Linux Essentials. This is the primary reading and lab resource for CSC 106. Enrolling via the invite link below places you in the correct section tied to this course.

CSC106 Spring 2026 – Linux Essentials Required

Opens: March 23, 2026  ·  Closes: May 23, 2026

Enroll in Linux Essentials ↗
What this course covers: Linux Essentials is an 18-chapter curriculum. CSC 106 assigns 8 specific chapters across the 8 weeks. In Week 1 you will read Chapters 12 and 13. You do not need to read the entire course — only the chapters assigned each week.
After enrolling: Verify you can see the course in your NetAcad dashboard before moving on. If the course shows as "not yet open," that is expected — it opens on March 23, 2026. You can still explore the course navigation and complete the NDG account linking step below before the course opens.

NDG Online is the virtual lab environment where Cisco labs run. Your NetAcad account and your NDG account must be linked before you can launch any lab. The process below walks you through the first-time link.

Use the same email address for your NDG account that you used for your Cisco Networking Academy account. You will need to verify the email address you choose, so make sure you have access to the inbox.
  1. 1
    Open the Linux Essentials course in NetAcad

    From your NetAcad dashboard, click on CSC106 Spring 2026 – Linux Essentials to enter the course.

  2. 2
    Find and launch "Before You Get Started"

    In the course table of contents, locate the activity titled Before You Get Started and click Launch. This activity is specifically designed to walk first-time users through the NDG linking process.

  3. 3
    Link or create your NDG account

    The activity will prompt you to connect an NDG account to the Cisco course.

    • If you already have an NDG account, enter your NDG credentials to link it.
    • If you do not have an NDG account, select the option to create one. Use the same email address as your NetAcad account.
  4. 4
    Verify your email address

    NDG will send a verification email to the address you provided. Open that email and click the verification link. You must complete this step before labs will work.

    Did not receive the verification email? Check your spam or junk folder. If it is not there after a few minutes, return to the NDG account creation page and request a new verification email.
  5. 5
    Confirm the link is working

    Return to the Linux Essentials course in NetAcad, navigate to the Chapter 13 lab, and verify that the Launch button opens the NDG lab environment without errors. If it opens successfully, you are fully set up.

These courses are not required. Enrolling in them now gives you access to supplemental reading and lab material that supports extra credit activities offered in several weeks throughout the semester. You will not fall behind or be penalized for not enrolling. If you are interested in deeper Linux knowledge or the LPIC-1 certification path, these are worth exploring.
