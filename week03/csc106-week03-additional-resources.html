Additional Resources

These free web resources supplement the Linux Essentials Lesson 13 review and provide the deeper systemd and cron content for this week. You do not need to read everything here—the Start here resources in the first two sections are enough to complete the required assignment. The remaining resources are available when you want more depth or are working on the extra credit.

systemd and systemctl

Start with the Beginner’s Guide to get the conceptual picture, then use the DigitalOcean tutorial for the specific systemctl commands you will run in the hands-on assignment. The Arch Wiki entry is there when you want a thorough reference beyond what either tutorial covers.

  • A Beginner’s Guide to Linux Services in Ubuntu: Mastering systemd and systemctl ↗ dev.to — Wycliffe Alphus
    Start here

    Excellent introduction to what systemd is and how you use systemctl to tell systemd what to do with services. If you are new to the concept of a service manager, read this one first.

  • How To Use Systemctl to Manage Systemd Services and Units on Ubuntu ↗ DigitalOcean
    Start here

    Practical, step-by-step guide to the systemctl commands you will use in the hands-on assignment—listing units, checking status, enabling, disabling, starting, and stopping services. Work through this before sitting down with the lab.

  • systemd ↗ Arch Wiki
    Go deeper

    One of the most thorough freely available references on systemd. Not Ubuntu-specific, but the concepts and commands transfer directly. Recommended when you want more depth than either of the tutorials above provides, or when you run into something the tutorials do not cover.

systemd Unit Files and Targets

This resource gives you the background on how unit files are structured and how the [Install] section connects a service to a target. It is useful context for understanding what systemctl enable is actually doing under the hood.

  • Understanding Systemd Units and Unit Files ↗ DigitalOcean

    Covers unit file structure in detail, including the [Unit], [Service], and [Install] sections, and explains the WantedBy= directive that connects a service to a target at boot. Good background for the hands-on assignment and useful context for the RSI Discussion.

Cron — Extra Credit and Week 7 Preparation

These resources support the optional Cron Job Setup extra credit assignment and give you a head start on Week 7, where a working cron job is a required part of the Server Configuration assignment. Work through the manpage first to understand the format, use Crontab Guru to build and test your schedule expression, then follow the DigitalOcean tutorial step by step on your VM.

  • crontab(5) Manual Page ↗ Ubuntu Manpages
    Reference

    The authoritative format reference for cron syntax. Explains every field, special characters (*, /, -, ,), and environment variables. Come back here when you need to know exactly what a field accepts or how a particular expression is evaluated.

  • Crontab Guru ↗ crontab.guru
    Interactive tool

    Paste or build a cron schedule expression and instantly see a plain-English description of when it runs. Use this to verify your expression before adding it to your crontab. Particularly useful when you are still learning the five-field format and want immediate feedback on what your schedule means.

  • Use Cron to Automate Tasks on Ubuntu: Complete Guide ↗ DigitalOcean
    Step-by-step

    Step-by-step tutorial for setting up, editing, and verifying a cron job on Ubuntu. Covers crontab -e, saving and listing entries with crontab -l, and confirming that the job ran. Follow this guide alongside the manpage for the extra credit assignment.
